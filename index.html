About Me

I am a Research Fellow at Monash University in the School of Physics and Astronomy, where I study astrophysics using gravitational waves. My research focuses on applying Bayesian statistics to address complex challenges in astrophysical inference. I am particularly fascinated by black holes, neutron stars—especially pulsars—and the gravitational-wave background, which never cease to amaze me.

I am also passionate about dark matter experiments and engineering marvels, such as gravitational-wave observatories, which push the boundaries of modern science and technology.

Before transitioning to astrophysics, I worked for several years at HP as an algorithm developer, bringing an industrial perspective to my research. I earned my PhD from Tel Aviv University, where I studied particle physics as part of the BaBar experiment.

I am a proud member of the BaBar Collaboration, the LIGO Scientific Collaboration, and OzGrav—the ARC Centre of Excellence for Gravitational-Wave Discovery.

Research

My work involves studying black holes, neutron stars, and binary mergers through gravitational wave observations. I specialize in Bayesian inference, hierarchical modeling, and statistical methods for analyzing data from LIGO and Virgo detectors.
